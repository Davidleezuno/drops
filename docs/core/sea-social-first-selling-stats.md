# Southeast Asia evidence: “Social-first selling is accelerating”

## Best slide-safe statistics

1. **Video commerce grew 5× in three years and reached 25% of Southeast Asia’s ecommerce GMV in 2025.**
   - **Year / geography:** 2022–2025; SEA-6 (Indonesia, Malaysia, the Philippines, Singapore, Thailand, and Vietnam).
   - **Definition:** Video commerce, described by the report as “shoppertainment”—a fusion of content and commerce, often creator-led. Its share of ecommerce GMV rose from **less than 5% in 2022** to an estimated **25% in 2025**.
   - **Source:** Google, Temasek, and Bain, *e-Conomy SEA 2025*, based on Cube data. The 2025 estimate is annualised from H1 2025. [Google summary](https://blog.google/company-news/inside-google/around-the-globe/google-asia/sea-economy-2025/) · [Full report (p. 15)](https://www.temasek.com.sg/content/dam/temasek-corporate/news-and-views/resources/reports/e-conomy-sea-2025-report.pdf)

2. **More than 3 million sellers and stores in SEA-6 used video to sell in 2025—up 80% year over year.**
   - **Year / geography:** 2025 estimate vs. 2024; SEA-6.
   - **Definition:** Sellers and stores using video commerce. This is the cleanest merchant-adoption evidence for the slide title.
   - **Source:** Google, Temasek, and Bain, *e-Conomy SEA 2025*, based on Cube data. [Full report (p. 15)](https://www.temasek.com.sg/content/dam/temasek-corporate/news-and-views/resources/reports/e-conomy-sea-2025-report.pdf)

3. **SEA-6 video-commerce GMV rose from US$6B in 2022 to US$46B in 2025; it grew 2.5× from 2023 to 2025.**
   - **Year / geography:** 2022–2025; SEA-6.
   - **Definition:** GMV attributed to video commerce: **US$6B (2022), US$18B (2023), US$31B (2024), US$46B (2025 estimate)**.
   - **Source:** Google, Temasek, and Bain, *e-Conomy SEA 2025*, based on Cube data. [Full report (p. 15)](https://www.temasek.com.sg/content/dam/temasek-corporate/news-and-views/resources/reports/e-conomy-sea-2025-report.pdf)

4. **Video-commerce transaction volume across SEA-6 increased 50% year over year in 2025.**
   - **Year / geography:** 2025 estimate vs. 2024; SEA-6.
   - **Definition:** Transaction count, not GMV. The report says growth is volume-led, with a typical video-commerce average order value of **US$6–7**, compared with **US$13–15** for ecommerce overall.
   - **Source:** Google, Temasek, and Bain, *e-Conomy SEA 2025*, based on Cube data. [Full report (p. 15)](https://www.temasek.com.sg/content/dam/temasek-corporate/news-and-views/resources/reports/e-conomy-sea-2025-report.pdf)

5. **Singapore’s video-commerce seller/store base grew 125% year over year to 80,000 in 2025.**
   - **Year / geography:** 2025 estimate vs. 2024; Singapore.
   - **Definition:** Sellers and stores using video commerce. Transaction volume also grew **30% year over year to 45 million**.
   - **Source:** Temasek’s official *e-Conomy SEA 2025* release, based on the Google/Temasek/Bain report. [Temasek release](https://www.temasek.com.sg/en/news-and-resources/news-room/news/2025/e-conomy-sea-2025-report-aseans-digital-economy-poised-to-surpass-300-billion)

6. **Indonesia’s video-commerce seller/store base grew 75% year over year to 800,000 in 2025.**
   - **Year / geography:** 2025 estimate vs. 2024; Indonesia.
   - **Definition:** Sellers and stores using video commerce. Transaction volume grew **90% year over year to 2.6 billion**, making Indonesia the region’s largest and fastest-growing video-commerce market in the report.
   - **Source:** Google Indonesia’s official *e-Conomy SEA 2025* release. [Google Indonesia](https://blog.google/intl/id-id/company-news/outreach-initiatives/e-conomy-sea-2025-indonesia-memiliki-pertumbuhan-video-commerce-terbesar-di-asia-tenggara/)

## Recommended slide treatment

Use these two together:

> **5×**  
> Video commerce growth in Southeast Asia since 2022

> **3M+**  
> Sellers and stores now use video to sell—**up 80% YoY**

Small source line:

> Google, Temasek & Bain, *e-Conomy SEA 2025* (SEA-6; 2025 annualised estimate)

If the audience is especially Singapore-focused, replace the first number with:

> **+125%**  
> Growth in Singapore sellers and stores using video commerce in 2025

## Scope and wording cautions

- **Video commerce is not identical to social commerce.** The report defines it as content-and-commerce “shoppertainment,” often creator-led. It supports the broader framing **“social-first selling is accelerating,”** but the exact measured category is video commerce.
- **Live selling is a subset, not the whole statistic.** Do not label the 25%, 5×, or 3M+ figures as “livestream commerce.”
- **These figures do not measure all off-platform social selling.** Informal orders that begin on TikTok, Instagram, or WhatsApp and settle elsewhere may not be captured.
- **GMV is ecommerce merchandise value, not HitPay payment volume or merchant revenue.**
- **The 2025 regional figures are estimates annualised from H1 2025.** Keep “2025 estimate” in speaker notes or the source line.
- TikTok/Accenture’s 2024 finding that **93% of surveyed APAC consumers wanted to maintain or increase shopping activity on content-driven platforms** is directionally useful, but it covers Indonesia, Thailand, Vietnam, Japan, and Korea—not SEA as a whole—and was commissioned/published by a platform vendor. Prefer the Google/Temasek/Bain figures on the main slide. [TikTok source](https://newsroom.tiktok.com/apac-consumer-report?lang=en-SG)
